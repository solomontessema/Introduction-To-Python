Data structures are the backbone of any program. They help you organize, store, and retrieve information efficiently. In Python, the most commonly used data structures are lists, dictionaries, tuples, and sets — each designed for different types of tasks.

Understanding these structures allows you to manage data like customer records, product catalogs, or survey results with clarity and control. For non-technical leaders, this means being able to interpret, manipulate, and communicate data more effectively.

---

### **Why Data Structures Matter**

Python’s built-in data structures help you:

- Group related information together  
- Access and update data quickly  
- Filter and sort large datasets  
- Build logic that reflects real-world relationships  

Whether you're managing a team roster or analyzing performance metrics, choosing the right structure makes your work faster and more reliable.

---

### **Common Python Structures**

- **Lists**: Ordered collections (like a to-do list)  
- **Dictionaries**: Key-value pairs (like a contact book)  
- **Tuples**: Fixed collections (like coordinates)  
- **Sets**: Unique items (like tags or categories)

Each structure has strengths depending on what you're trying to achieve.
